# Infinitive Parsing

*Chapter 32 — Infinitive*

A 20-item parsing drill on Greek infinitives. Students parse each form (tense / voice / lexical form), identify the use (complementary, articular/noun, purpose, result, indirect discourse, temporal), and translate. Items are drawn from GNT-style sentences. An answer key is embedded in all formats.

## Coverage

| Use | Count | Items |
|---|---|---|
| Complementary infinitive | 5 | #1–#5 |
| Articular infinitive (τό + inf.) | 3 | #6–#8 |
| Purpose (εἰς τό / πρός τό / τοῦ) | 4 | #9–#12 |
| Result (ὥστε + inf.) | 3 | #13–#15 |
| Indirect discourse (acc. + inf.) | 3 | #16–#18 |
| Temporal (ἐν τῷ + inf.) | 2 | #19–#20 |

## Sample Questions

**Q1.** 1 θέλω λύειν τοὺς δούλους.
> **A:** Pres · Act · λύω · Complementary · "I want to loose the slaves."

**Q2.** 2 οὐκ εἰμὶ ἱκανὸς λῦσαι τὸν ἱμάντα τοῦ ὑποδήματος αὐτοῦ.
> **A:** Aor · Act · λύω · Complementary · "I am not worthy to loose the strap of his sandal." (cf. Mark 1:7)

**Q3.** 3 ἤρξατο διδάσκειν αὐτοὺς πολλά.
> **A:** Pres · Act · διδάσκω · Complementary · "He began to teach them many things."
