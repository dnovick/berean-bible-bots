# Passage Exercise

*Chapter 19 — Qal Pronominal Suffixes on Verbs*

[Full Screen (Interactive)](ch19-passage-exercise.html){.md-button .md-button--primary}  [Print / PDF](ch19-passage-exercise.pdf){.md-button}  [Markdown](ch19-passage-exercise.md){.md-button}

This passage exercise presents 14 highlighted verb+suffix forms drawn from five canonical passages: Genesis 28:15 (God's promise to Jacob at Bethel), Genesis 45:4–8 (Joseph's theological reframing), Psalm 23:1–4 (the LORD my shepherd), Genesis 39:19–20 (Potiphar and the Inf.Const. temporal clauses), and Psalm 16:1 + Deuteronomy 31:6 (imperative and imperfect + suffix). Students identify the base conjugation and root, parse the suffix PGN, determine whether the suffix is a direct object (on finite verbs) or subject (on Infinitive Construct), and provide the full gloss.

## Sample Questions

**Q1.** 1 וּשְׁמַרְתִּיךָ
> **A:** Qal Weqatal 1cs (שמר) · 2ms · Object · "and I will keep you" — God's promise; suffix replaces אֹתְךָ

**Q2.** 2 וַהֲשִׁבֹתִיךָ
> **A:** Hiphil Weqatal 1cs (שוב) · 2ms · Object · "and I will bring you back" — Hiphil causative "cause to return" + 2ms suffix

**Q3.** 3 אֶעֱזָבְךָ
> **A:** Qal Imperfect 1cs (עזב) · 2ms · Object · "I will forsake you" — with לֹא = "I will NOT forsake you"; I-aleph gutt.
