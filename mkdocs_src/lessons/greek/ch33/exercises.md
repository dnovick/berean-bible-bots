# Ch33 — Imperative: Exercises

[← Back to lesson](index.md)

| Exercise | Description |
|---|---|
| [Imperative Parsing](exercises/ch33-imperative-parsing/index.md) | A 20-item parsing drill on the Greek imperative. Students parse each form (tense / voice / person / number / mood / lexical form), note aspect implications, and translate. Items cover present and aorist imperatives (active, middle/passive), 2nd and 3rd person forms, prohibitions, and the imperative of εἰμί. An answer key is embedded in all formats. |
| [Prohibition Drill](exercises/ch33-prohibition-drill/index.md) | A 20-item pattern drill presenting prohibition sentences drawn from or modeled on GNT passages. Students classify each prohibition as STOP (μή + present imperative = stop an ongoing action) or DONT (μή + aorist subjunctive = do not begin), parse tense and mood of the verbal form, supply the lexical form, and translate. The exercise includes exactly 10 of each pattern and includes a μι-verb (δίδωμι, #19) and two φοβέομαι items (#2, #11) that directly contrast the two patterns with the same lexical verb. Discussion questions probe the limits of the STOP/DONT rule, μι-verb imperative morphology, and pragmatic differences between the two prohibition strategies. An answer key is embedded in all three formats. |
