Discussion Questions

  1. Items 1 (נָכוֹן) and 2 (נָסַב) have the same prefix vowel (נָ, qamets) and nearly identical shapes. What is the only reliable way to know that נָכוֹן is Biconsonantal (root כּוּן) and נָסַב is Geminate (root סָבַב)?
  2. Items 9 (הֵקִים) and 10 (הֵסֵב) both have הֵ prefix (tsere). One retains a medial vowel letter and the other does not. Explain in one sentence how that difference reveals the class.
  3. Part C includes roots מוּג, רוּם, and שׁוּב — none covered directly in the drill. How do you apply the Biconsonantal/Geminate diagnostic to an unfamiliar root? What is the one question you must ask?
  4. Items 22 (הוּרַם) is Hophal, not Niphal or Hiphil. What prefix vowel distinguishes Hophal from Hiphil in the perfect?
