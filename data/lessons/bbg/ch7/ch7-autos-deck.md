## How to Use

- **Front of card:** inflected Greek form
- **Back of card:** part of speech + gender(s) + case + number + gloss
- Shared forms (e.g. αὐτοῦ = masc and neut gen sg) list all valid parsings
- No vocative — pronouns do not have a vocative in Greek

> **Three uses of αὐτός:**
> 1. **Personal pronoun** — standing alone: *he, she, it, they* (NT frequency: 5,597×)
> 2. **Intensive** — in predicate position with a noun (no article between): *himself, herself, itself, themselves*
> 3. **Identifying adjective** — in attributive position (between article and noun): *the same*
>
> All three uses share the same paradigm below. The distinction is positional, not morphological.
